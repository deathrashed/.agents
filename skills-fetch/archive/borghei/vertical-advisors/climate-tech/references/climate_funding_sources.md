# Climate Funding Sources

Climate-tech rarely gets funded by equity alone. The successful playbook blends grants, tax credits, equity, and (later) project finance. This guide is the menu.

---

## US Federal Grants and Loans

### DOE (Department of Energy)
- **ARPA-E:** Early-stage transformational research (typical $0.5M-$10M, multi-year)
- **DOE Loan Programs Office (LPO):** Loans/loan guarantees for at-scale clean energy projects ($50M-$3B)
- **Industrial Demonstrations Program:** Decarbonization of hard-to-abate industries
- **Hydrogen Hubs / Hydrogen Shot:** Hydrogen ecosystem investment
- **Carbon-removal demonstrations:** Direct air capture hubs, etc.

### USDA (Agriculture)
- **Rural Energy for America Program (REAP):** Rural renewable energy
- **Conservation Innovation Grants (CIG):** Soil carbon, regenerative ag
- **Various block-grant programs to states for climate-smart ag**

### NSF, NIH, NASA
- Standard research grants relevant to specific climate science

### SBIR / STTR
- Small business research grants ($150k Phase I, $1.5M Phase II)
- Available across DOE, USDA, NSF, NASA, EPA
- Founder-friendly for technical / R&D-heavy startups

### EPA
- Greenhouse Gas Reduction Fund (GGRF) — $27B from IRA
- Distributed via state Green Banks and other intermediaries

## US Tax Credits (IRA — Inflation Reduction Act)

The IRA dramatically restructured climate tax credits in 2022-2024. Key provisions:

### Section 45 (Production Tax Credit, PTC)
- Per-kWh credit for renewable electricity production
- Wind, solar, geothermal, others

### Section 48 (Investment Tax Credit, ITC)
- % of project cost as credit
- Solar, storage, others

### Section 45Y / 48E (Tech-neutral credits, post-2025)
- Replacing 45/48 for projects placed in service post-2025
- Tech-neutral as long as life-cycle emissions are zero

### Section 45Q (Carbon sequestration)
- Per-ton credit for sequestered CO2
- Higher rates for direct air capture (DAC)

### Section 45V (Hydrogen)
- Per-kg credit for clean hydrogen production
- Tiered by carbon intensity

### Section 45X (Advanced manufacturing)
- Per-unit credit for domestic manufacturing of clean tech components (solar, wind, storage)

### Sections 25C / 25D / 25E (Residential)
- Credits for residential efficiency, solar, EVs

### Section 30D / 25E / 30C (EVs)
- New / used / commercial EV credits, charging infrastructure

**Critical mechanic:** Direct pay (refundable) for tax-exempt entities and transferability for tax-paying entities created a market for tax credit transfers. Climate-tech projects can monetize credits without taking equity from tax-equity investors directly.

**Risk:** IRA credits can be modified by future Congresses. Building IRA-only business models is risk; building credit-amplified business models (where the project pencils without credit but credit accelerates) is more durable.

## EU Funding

### EU Innovation Fund
- Funded from EU ETS revenues; large grants for innovative low-carbon technologies
- Focus areas vary by call; typical sizes €5M-€100M+

### Horizon Europe
- EU's flagship research and innovation program
- Many climate-relevant calls

### EIB / EIF (European Investment Bank / Fund)
- Loans, equity, blended finance for climate projects

### Member-state programs
- France 2030, Germany's KfW, UK's Innovate UK, etc.

### CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)
- Not funding directly, but creates customer demand for reporting tools and verified emission reductions
- Effective 2024-2026 in tranches

## Other Public / Quasi-Public Sources

- California (Cap-and-trade revenue, GO-Biz programs, CEC)
- New York (NYSERDA), Massachusetts (Mass CEC), Washington
- Many states have green banks

### Multilateral (developing markets)
- World Bank, IFC, regional development banks
- Green Climate Fund (UNFCCC mechanism)

### Prizes
- XPRIZE (Carbon Removal $100M)
- Earthshot Prize
- Various private prizes

## Private Capital

### Climate-focused VC
- **Generalist climate funds:** Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Lowercarbon Capital, Energy Impact Partners, Clean Energy Ventures, World Fund (EU)
- **Tech-specific:** DCVC, Khosla Ventures, USV, others
- **Corporate venture:** Saudi Aramco, Shell Ventures, Chevron Technology Ventures, BP Ventures, AB InBev, Microsoft Climate Innovation Fund

### Climate-focused growth equity / private equity
- Generation Investment Management, Galvanize Climate Solutions, TPG Rise Climate, Ardian

### Catalytic / blended finance
- Stripe Climate, Frontier (advanced market commitment for CDR)
- Microsoft offtake agreements
- Climate-aligned offtake-finance vehicles

### Project finance (later stage)
- Once a project is bankable: tax-equity, senior debt, mezzanine
- Specialized lenders: Hannon Armstrong, NextEra Energy, others

## Funding Stack by Stage

### Pre-seed / Seed
- SBIR Phase I
- ARPA-E for hard-tech
- Climate-focused angel
- Climate VC (some funds invest at this stage)

### Series A
- Climate VC
- Some corporate venture
- Strategic partner equity

### Series B / C
- Climate growth equity
- Catalytic capital for first-of-a-kind plants
- DOE LPO for at-scale loan financing

### First commercial plant
- DOE LPO loans
- Tax credit transfer financing
- Project-finance debt and equity
- Strategic partners (utilities, industrial offtakers)

### Subsequent plants
- Standard project finance
- Possibly REIT / yieldco models

## Common Mistakes

1. **VC-only mindset.** Most climate categories are not pure-venture-fundable. Plan grant + venture + project-finance from start.
2. **Underestimating grant timeline.** DOE / EU grants take 6-18 months from application to award. Plan accordingly.
3. **Grant capacity.** Grants require writing skill; founders often spend 30-40% of their time on grant ops at certain stages.
4. **Over-relying on subsidy.** Build for unit economics that work at modest subsidy; subsidy upside makes it accelerate.
5. **Not stacking incentives.** Many projects can stack federal IRA + state + utility + corporate — failing to layer leaves money on the table.
6. **Missing offtake structure.** Hard-tech climate companies often need an offtake agreement to unlock project finance. Plan for it from Series B onwards.
