# Ecommerce Fulfillment Models

Most brands change fulfillment models 2-3 times between zero and $50M revenue. The model that works at $5K/month doesn't work at $5M/month. This guide is the decision framework.

---

## DTC Self-Fulfillment

**What:** You hold inventory, pick/pack/ship in-house.

**When it fits:**
- Pre-product-market-fit (need to talk to customers via the unboxing experience)
- Heavily customized products (custom orders, personalization)
- Very low volume (< 50 orders/month)
- Founders who want to feel each customer interaction

**Pros:**
- Maximum control over experience and packaging
- Direct customer feedback (you literally hear complaints when you pack)
- Cheapest at very small scale
- No 3PL minimums or contracts

**Cons:**
- Doesn't scale past ~500 orders/month without consuming all your time
- Real estate, equipment, hiring overhead
- Single point of failure (you / your team get sick, shipping stops)

---

## 3PL (Third-Party Logistics)

**What:** A 3PL warehouse holds your inventory and fulfills orders for you.

**Examples:** ShipBob, Shippo Fulfillment, Deliverr (Walmart-owned), Stord, Cart.com, regional 3PLs

**When it fits:**
- 200-50,000+ orders/month
- Standard SKUs (no excessive customization)
- Multi-channel sellers (DTC site + Amazon + wholesale)
- Need geographic distribution (multiple warehouses for shipping speed)

**Pros:**
- Scalable
- Multiple channel fulfillment from one inventory pool
- Often integrated with Shopify, BigCommerce, etc.
- Can reduce shipping time/cost via multi-warehouse footprint

**Cons:**
- Per-order pick/pack fees ($2-$6 typical)
- Storage fees ($25-$60 per pallet/month)
- Inbound receiving fees
- Onboarding takes 4-8 weeks for first integration
- Customer-experience control reduced (packaging, inserts, returns)

**Watch for:**
- Hidden fees (handling, returns processing, special projects)
- Long-term contract minimums
- Off-the-bat 3PL switching cost (re-shipping inventory)

## Amazon FBA (Fulfillment by Amazon)

**What:** Amazon holds inventory, fulfills Amazon orders + sometimes off-Amazon orders.

**When it fits:**
- Amazon-channel-focused brands
- Smaller, lighter products (storage cost favors them)
- Products that benefit from Prime badge (price-sensitive categories)

**Pros:**
- Prime badge boosts conversion
- Customer-service handled by Amazon
- Multi-channel fulfillment (MCF) — Amazon ships your DTC orders too (premium pricing)
- No upfront warehouse build

**Cons:**
- Long-term storage fees (especially for slow movers)
- Inbound restrictions and rejections
- Inventory controlled by Amazon (you can't easily pull and use elsewhere)
- Categories dominated by Amazon — you compete with Amazon Basics
- Suspension risk: a single account issue can pause your business

**Watch for:**
- IPI (Inventory Performance Index) thresholds — low IPI restricts inbound
- Off-platform Amazon ads — costs eat margin quickly
- Mixed-condition issues — your inventory can be commingled with similar inventory

## Dropshipping

**What:** Supplier fulfills directly to your customer; you don't hold inventory.

**Examples:** AliExpress dropship, Spocket, supplier-direct programs

**When it fits:**
- Testing product-market fit before investing in inventory
- Long-tail SKU testing
- Cash-flow-constrained launches

**Pros:**
- Near-zero inventory risk
- Wide catalog testing
- Low working capital

**Cons:**
- Low margin (supplier takes their cut)
- Quality control near-zero
- Long shipping times (dropshipping from Asia to NA = weeks)
- Customer experience suffers
- Returns are operationally painful
- Very competitive (everyone can sell the same thing)

**Reality:** Most dropship brands stay small or transition to holding their own inventory once they find a winning SKU.

---

## Wholesale / Retail

**What:** You sell to retailers (wholesale) who sell to end customers; or directly to retail buyer.

**When it fits:**
- Brand has built DTC traction worth bringing to retail
- Product fits retail merchandising (strong brand, clear category, retail packaging)
- Brand can absorb wholesale margin compression (brands typically take 40-50% of MSRP at wholesale)

**Pros:**
- Bigger market reach
- Brand validation (you're "in stores")
- Cash flow can be better than DTC (retailer pays on net 30/60/90 terms; no CAC on each sale)

**Cons:**
- Margin compression (wholesale price = MSRP × 0.40-0.50)
- Retailer can drop you with little notice
- Inventory commitments and EDI integration overhead
- Trade marketing (slotting fees, promotional support, co-op marketing)

---

## Hybrid Models (Most Brands)

Most successful ecommerce brands run multiple models simultaneously:

**Example (~$10M brand):**
- DTC site self-fulfilled or 3PL — best margins, customer data
- Amazon FBA — discovery channel, Prime badge for price-sensitive
- Wholesale to specialty retail — brand-building, niche reach
- Possibly: pop-up retail or wholesale-mass-retail attempts

**Pitfalls of hybrid:**
- Inventory allocation across channels (Amazon FBA inventory can't ship DTC easily)
- Channel conflict (price differences confuse customers)
- Margin attribution complexity (which channel actually drives the order?)

## Decision Heuristic

| Stage | Default fulfillment |
|-------|---------------------|
| 0-100 orders/month | Self-fulfill |
| 100-1,000 orders/month | Self-fulfill or single 3PL |
| 1,000-10,000 orders/month | Single 3PL + Amazon FBA |
| 10,000-100,000 orders/month | Multi-warehouse 3PL + Amazon FBA |
| 100,000+ orders/month | Owned warehouse OR enterprise 3PL |

These are heuristics. Specific product (size, weight, fragility) and channel mix change the answer.

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Premature 3PL.** Signing a 3PL contract before validating product-market fit means paying minimums on inventory that doesn't move.
- **Single-channel fulfillment.** Amazon FBA only works for Amazon orders; FBA inventory in customer hands is hard to redirect.
- **Underestimating returns.** Returns are 10-30% of orders in some categories. Reverse logistics cost can equal forward.
- **Sticky 3PL choice.** Switching 3PLs takes 2-4 months. Pick well; renegotiate before switching.
- **No SKU rationalization.** A long tail of slow-moving SKUs at a 3PL bleeds storage fees.
