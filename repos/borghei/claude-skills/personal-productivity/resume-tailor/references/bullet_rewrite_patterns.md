# Bullet Rewrite Patterns

Resume bullets should be **action-led, quantified, and outcome-focused**. The patterns below convert generic responsibility statements into impact bullets that survive ATS keyword filters and recruiter skim-reads.

---

## The CAR Pattern

Every strong bullet contains three elements:

| Element | What it answers | Example phrase |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Challenge** | What problem existed? | "Faced a rising 30% checkout abandonment rate…" |
| **Action** | What did *you* do? | "Designed and shipped a one-page checkout…" |
| **Result** | What changed, in numbers? | "Cut abandonment to 12% within 60 days." |

**Compressed CAR (resume format):**
> Cut checkout abandonment from 30% to 12% in 60 days by shipping a redesigned one-page flow.

---

## Action-Verb Library

Group verbs by intent. Use one per bullet — never two.

**Built / shipped:** built, shipped, launched, deployed, delivered, released, rolled out, productionized
**Improved / optimized:** improved, reduced, accelerated, streamlined, optimized, increased, doubled, tripled, halved
**Led / owned:** led, owned, drove, headed, directed, managed, mentored, coached
**Discovered / analyzed:** analyzed, identified, diagnosed, investigated, audited, surveyed, profiled
**Designed / decided:** designed, architected, defined, established, structured, scoped, prioritized
**Influenced / convinced:** influenced, persuaded, secured, negotiated, presented, pitched, won

Avoid: "responsible for", "helped with", "involved in", "worked on", "participated in", "assisted with".

---

## Quantification Cheat Sheet

Quantify with one of:

- **Percentage change:** "increased trial-to-paid conversion 27%"
- **Absolute scale:** "across 14 markets and 3 product lines"
- **Time saved:** "cutting weekly reporting from 8 hours to 30 minutes"
- **Money:** "$240k annual cost reduction"
- **Volume:** "processing 12M events/day"
- **Headcount / ownership:** "led a team of 6 across 3 time zones"

If you cannot quantify, use a **qualitative landmark**: "first integration of its kind in the org", "approved by the CTO", "selected as the company-wide standard".

---

## Weak-Phrase Blacklist

Run a search for these and rewrite every match:

- Responsible for
- Helped with
- Involved in
- Worked on / Worked with
- Participated in
- Assisted with
- Contributed to
- Was part of a team that
- Tasked with
- Duties included

---

## Before / After Examples

**Before:** Responsible for the deployment pipeline.
**After:** Cut deploy time from 45 min to 6 min by migrating CI to a multi-stage parallel pipeline.

**Before:** Helped with onboarding new engineers.
**After:** Designed a 30-day onboarding program adopted across 4 teams; new hires shipped first PR in week 1.

**Before:** Involved in customer research.
**After:** Ran 22 customer interviews that produced the discovery signals behind two roadmap features.

**Before:** Worked on improving SEO.
**After:** Grew organic search traffic 64% YoY by restructuring 200+ landing pages and rebuilding internal linking.

---

## Bullet Length

- **Target:** 16-22 words.
- **Maximum:** 28 words.
- **Anything longer** belongs in two bullets — or is hiding fluff.

---

## Order Within a Role

Within a single job, list bullets in this priority:

1. Highest-impact outcome with money / scale
2. Cross-functional or leadership scope
3. Technical depth / specialization
4. Recognition (awards, promotions, internal selection)

If a bullet doesn't fit one of these four, cut it.
